Three ways to read

Same story. Three sizes.

Every book in the library is written at three reading levels. Pick the one that fits your reader today — and slide up as they grow.

Ages 3–5

Little Listener

Short, warm stories with rhythm and repetition. Made to be read aloud. About 200 words.

Ages 5–7

New Reader

Early chapter-style stories with characters that grow and learn. About 500 words.

Ages 7–10

On Your Own

Richer stories with dialogue, deeper themes, and open questions to think about. About 1,000 words.

Or make your own

A fresh story, written just for them.

Tell us what the story should be about. We'll ask your reader's level, then write something new — in about twenty seconds.

  1. Describe the story.

    A topic, a character's name, an optional lesson to tuck inside.

  2. Pick a reading level.

    Little Listener · New Reader · On Your Own.

  3. Read it. Print it. Keep it.

    Your story lives in your browser. Nothing is saved on our side.

Why we built this

Storytelling is the oldest way of teaching.

Every story here has a small lesson tucked inside — never preached, never on the surface. We built Fablewick because the things that make children kind, brave, patient, and curious have always traveled by story. We didn't want to put a paywall between a kid and a quiet evening.

Free, forever

No ads. No subscriptions. No premium tier. Donations are optional, never required.

Open source

The whole library lives in a public repo. If you write a story, you can contribute it.

Built for trust

No accounts. No tracking. No data collection from children. COPPA-safe by design.

AI as a feature

The library comes first. AI generation is one small workshop in a much bigger room.
